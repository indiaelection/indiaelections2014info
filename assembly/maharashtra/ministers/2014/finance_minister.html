Sudhir Mungantiwar is the Maharashtra's Ex chief of Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). He is currently a cabinet minister of finance and planning, and forest departments in the Govt of Maharashtra.

He started his public life at the age of seventeen when he was appointed as Secretary of Chaatrasangh Sardar Patel Mahavidyalaya in 1979. He was appointed the secretary of BJP in 2014 from Chandrapur. In 1989 and 1991 he unsuccessfully contested the Lok Sabha elections from Chandrapur. In 1993, he was appointed as Vice President of Bharatiya Janata Youth Movement's state unit.He was elected MLA from Chandrapur thrice and in 2009 from Ballarpur. He set a record, winning for the first time in 1995 with a margin of 55,000 votes. He served as the Minister for Tourism and Consumer Protection Department during the Shiv Sena-BJP saffron alliance. In 1998, he was appointed General Secretary of BJP's state unit. In 1998, he received the best orator's award for his performance in Vidhan Sabha. In 1999, he was made a minister in the Sena-BJP government.In 1999 and 2004, he was reelected from Chandrapur. In the October 2009 elections, he contested from Ballarpur and was elected for the fourth time. He won the G.L. Nardekar Memorial Award for Legislative struggle in the field of blind & handicap persons. Because of his Legislative efforts for Gondwana University for Chandrapur & Gadchiroli Districts Govt. form an ad hoc committee of Vidhansabha.
